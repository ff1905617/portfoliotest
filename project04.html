Dissecting How You Feel

Objective:

Survey Massart Students and determine their stress levels based on four metrics.


Tools:

HTML, CSS and Javascript


Role:

Data analyst, developer and video editor


Duration:

October 2024-December 2024

What's the story?

College can be stressful, especially when you're trying to balance commuting, work and getting the most out of your education. I decided to create an interactive project to showcase the average stress levels of students at Massart. Each student's stress level is based on a stress score. The higher the score, the more stressed they are. The stress score of each student or "subject" is calculated using the following four metrics:

  • Point one: How many hours of sleep do you get a night?
  • Point two: Are you happy in your major? (1-10)
  • Point three: Financial stress (1-10)
  • Point four: Is Massart preparing you for the future?

Because of the nature of the data, point one and point two are inverted so that 10 = 0 and 1 = 10. Also, the answers yes, no and maybe are translated to 1, 10 and 5 in order to quantify them.

(p1 + p2 + p3 + p4) / 4 = stress score

In the interactive data set, there are three states: healthy, warning and danger. Each state correlates to a stress score range:

  • 1-4 Healthy
  • 5-7 Warning
  • 8-10 Danger

The user interacts with the data by clicking a subject on the list. Their correalting stress status and stats are displayed as well as a gif of a heart, also representing their score. Check out the interactive project using the button below!

The Process - Week One

This project was a five week endeavor. It took several different forms over the course of my research before I eventually settled on my final concept and executed. During the first week, I started with a set of 21 questions. I walked around Massachusetts College of Art and Design, soliciting other students for interviews. After collecting my data, I sifted through it and found that the questions that interested me the most had to do with students' well-being anxieties because I could relate to them.

  • 1. How many hours of sleep do you get a night?
  • 2. Are you happy in your major? (1-10)
  • 3. Financial stress (1-10)
  • 4. Is Massart preparing you for the future?
  • 5. Do you worry more about the past, present or future?

Week Two

During the second week of my research, I made a "bird's eye view" poster to organize my thinking. I created a visualization for the following question correllations:

  • Major size and happiness in major
  • Is Massart preparing you for the future, Do you think about the past, present or future the most? and hours of sleep
  • Financial stress (1-10) and Do you live on or off campus?

Week Three

The third week of the project brought about some doubt regarding my concept. I wanted to try something new so I took a look at some of the other data in the collection. One of the survey questions was "Have you ever had a paranormal experience? If so, what happened?" I took a look at the answers and was very intruiged. I decided to make an interactive collection of ghost stories. The iteration took the shape of a digital deck of cards. Each card can be flipped over and has a ghost story on the back. The cards cycle infinitely and can be shuffled with the click of a button.

Week Four, Five & Six

For the final weeks of the project, I ended up returning to my original concept. I thought it would be more important to bring to light students' stress rather than to continue with my ghost story idea. While some stress is good, prolonged and intense stress can be harmful, primarily to the heart. High blood pressure, irregular heartbeat and adrenaline spikes are all symptoms of chronic stress. Because of this, I wanted to pair my data with a visual of a human heart.

In order to quantify the stress, I used the data and equation I cited at the beginning of the case study. This made it easy to assign different states to all of the subjects. In order to make the project interactive, I had to find a way to allow the user to click on a given subject and have the data points and stress status display on the page. I used HTML data attributes to store hidden data in each div and display it in panels 1-4 when clicked. I created Javascript constants for all of the data attributes and used an Event Listener to print each value from the data attributes in the DOM on click.
